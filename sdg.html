Global Impact

Aligning Financial Inclusion with Sustainable Development

Saakh empowers informal merchants and micro-entrepreneurs. By bridging the informal economy and formal banking, we support the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

8
SDG 8: Decent Work & Economic Growth

Promoting Formalization and Credit Access for MSMEs

Over 90% of India's workforce is informal, locking roadside vendors out of institutional loans. Saakh allows fruits and vegetable sellers, kiranas, and tea shops to extract credit credentials directly from their everyday cash logs (like UPI receipts & registers), directly helping formalize and grow micro-enterprises (Target 8.3).

9
SDG 9: Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure

Integrating Small-Scale Enterprises into Value Chains

Target 9.3 prioritizes expanding access to financial services and credit for small-scale enterprises. Saakh uses AI-driven parsing to clean unstructured transaction histories, transforming them into reliable credit markers. This lowers the lending risk for local microfinance institutions.

1
SDG 1: No Poverty

Equal Access to Financial Services and Microfinance

Eradicating poverty requires equal access to financial services and microfinance (Target 1.4). Underbanked entrepreneurs often depend on predatory local lenders with high interest rates. Saakh generates clean, audit-ready statements that help them leverage safe micro-loans.

10
SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

Democratizing Financial Visibility for the Underbanked

We reduce systemic financial inequality by making the credit assessment bottom-up. No matter how small the business, a local merchant can translate everyday UPI logs into high-fidelity indicators of creditworthiness, ensuring equal opportunities for economic progress.
